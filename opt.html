Naturalization Applicants

N-400 applications from Iranian nationals are subject to the pause. Oath ceremonies and final decisions are delayed. While your application is pending, you remain a lawful permanent resident with all associated rights.

Oath ceremonies for Iranians: no exception

For N-400 applicants who had previously been approved and had oath ceremonies scheduled, the exception that allows oath ceremonies to proceed during the pause does not apply to Iranian nationals. As of April 20, 2026, Iranians whose ceremonies were scheduled and subsequently canceled cannot complete their naturalization — even though applicants from non-designated countries may still proceed under this exception.5

N-600 for U.S. citizen parents

If you are a U.S. citizen and your child holds a green card and is under the age of 21, Form N-600 (Application for Certificate of Citizenship) is not subject to the pause.5

You remain a permanent resident

While your naturalization application is pending, you remain a lawful permanent resident with all associated rights.4

What you should do

  • Keep documentation of continuous residence and physical presence in the U.S.
  • Do not commit any criminal offenses. Any conviction can derail your application or jeopardize your green card.
  • Do not travel for extended periods — extensive travel can call into question continuous residence.
