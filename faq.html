OPT & STEM OPT (Optional Practical Training)

If you applied for OPT or a STEM OPT extension on or before October 30, 2025, your auto-extension may still cover you. If you applied later, the rules changed.

How the pause affects OPT

OPT applications (Form I-765) are subject to the adjudication pause. You can still file, and USCIS will accept your application, but final decisions are not being issued.

Automatic extension rules

The rules for automatic work permit extensions changed on October 30, 2025, when DHS issued a new interim final rule ending the automatic-extension program for most categories.3

Filing dateAutomatic-extension status
Before Oct 30, 2025Eligible for up to 540-day automatic extension. You can continue working with proof of your pending application.
On or after Oct 30, 2025Most applicants no longer receive an automatic extension. You cannot work without a final USCIS decision.

Initial OPT (not a renewal) has never qualified for the 540-day automatic extension. If your initial OPT application is pending, you cannot begin working until USCIS issues your EAD.

STEM OPT extensions

If you timely filed your 24-month STEM OPT extension, you may be eligible for a 180-day automatic extension of your current work authorization. However, your authorization expires at the end of 180 days if USCIS has not issued a decision. Due to the pause, many students are at risk of reaching this limit.4

The automatic 180-day extension of work authorization for timely-filed STEM OPT extensions continues to operate normally and is not affected by the adjudication pause.13

Court developments

On April 17, 2026, a federal court in Behdin v. Edlow, No. 5:26-cv-00566 (N.D. Cal.), issued a preliminary injunction requiring USCIS to adjudicate certain Form I-765 applications. This ruling is limited to the 32 named plaintiffs in that case and does not automatically extend to other applicants. However, it may provide legal grounds for similarly situated individuals to pursue their own litigation.5

What you should do

  • Communicate with your Designated School Official (DSO) immediately about your situation.
  • Keep all documentation: USCIS receipts, I-20 form, proof of enrollment, correspondence.
  • Know your critical deadlines: I-20 expiration, OPT expiration, STEM OPT 180-day limit.
  • Explore options: SEVIS transfer, CPT placement, or other authorization.
  • If your status is at risk, consult an immigration attorney about mandamus litigation.
