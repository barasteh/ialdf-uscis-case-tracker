H-1B Work Visa

H-1B holders benefit from two protections most other visa types do not have during the pause: the 240-day extension rule for timely-filed extensions, and full portability to a new employer.

The 240-day extension rule

If your H-1B extension is pending and you filed a timely extension request before your current H-1B expired, you may continue working for your current employer for up to 240 days past your expiration date. This only applies if you filed timely — if your H-1B has already expired without a pending extension, this rule does not protect you.9

Portability

H-1B holders specifically benefit from portability, which allows you to transfer to a new employer without being affected by the pause. H-1B portability operates under a separate statutory framework that does not require the pending petition to be adjudicated before you begin work for the new employer. This provision does not apply to other visa classifications such as O-1 or L-1.13

Applications to change from one immigration status to another — for example, from F-1 to H-1B — are not being adjudicated during the pause. If you are currently maintaining valid status in one category, consider preserving that status rather than applying for a change that will not be processed.13

EAD categories & auto-extension

The same October 30, 2025 rule change affects EAD holders in employment-based categories. Check your I-797C notice for your eligibility category:10

  • A03 = Refugees · A05 = Asylees · A10 = Withholding of Removal
  • C08 = Pending Asylum · C09 = Pending Adjustment of Status
  • C26 = H-4 Dependent Spouses · C31 = VAWA Self-Petitioners

Categories that were previously eligible for auto-extension lost that protection on October 30, 2025 for renewals filed on or after that date. Current exceptions: TPS beneficiaries (A12/C19) if announced via Federal Register, STEM OPT recipients (180-day extension), and spouses of L and E visa holders.
