Employment-Based Green Card

PERM, I-140, and I-485 adjudications are paused. AC21 portability — the ability to change employers without losing your place in line — is your most important tool during the wait.

AC21 portability

If your employer filed an I-140 petition and it has been approved, and you have a pending I-485 that has been pending for at least 180 days, you may be able to change employers without losing your place in line for the green card. This is called “portability” under AC21 (American Competitiveness in the Twenty-First Century Act).11

This gives you flexibility during the pause — you are not locked to the employer that sponsored you.

Two boxes have to be checked

AC21 portability requires both an approved I-140 and an I-485 that has been pending 180+ days. If your I-140 has not been approved, or your I-485 has been pending under 180 days, portability does not yet apply. Talk to immigration counsel before making any employer change.

Risk to watch: pending I-485 + expired underlying status

If your I-485 is currently pending and your underlying status has expired or is about to expire, you should be aware of a significant change in enforcement practice. Under prior administrations, filing an I-485 while your underlying status was valid meant you were not accruing unlawful presence while the application remained pending, even if the underlying status subsequently expired. ICE is no longer consistently following this precedent. There have been cases where Iranians with pending I-485 applications — who are not yet green card holders — were approached by ICE, detained, and placed in removal proceedings after their underlying status expired. If your underlying status is expiring while your I-485 is pending, consult an immigration attorney immediately.13
