F-1 Student Status

Your F-1 status continues as long as you maintain a full course of study and comply with your school’s requirements. A delay in an OPT application does not by itself terminate your F-1 status.

SEVIS transfers continue

You can still transfer your SEVIS record from one school to another. The pause primarily affects final decisions on benefit requests, not the SEVIS management system itself.7 This includes transfers for Day-1 CPT after OPT ends, which involve a SEVIS record transfer rather than a USCIS benefit application.13

Duration of status

F-1 students are currently admitted for “duration of status,” which means your authorized period of stay is tied to your academic program rather than a fixed calendar date. You do not need to file a status extension as long as you remain enrolled in your program. However, a new rule is expected (not yet effective as of April 2026) that would assign F-1 holders a specific expiration date instead of duration of status.13

Curricular Practical Training (CPT)

If you are currently enrolled and a CPT option is available through your program, you can activate it.

CPT trade-off you should know about

Completing 12 months or more of full-time CPT disqualifies you from Optional Practical Training (OPT). Discuss this trade-off with your DSO before enrolling in CPT.14

60-day grace period

Be aware of the 60-day grace period — the time you can remain in the U.S. after your program ends without other valid status. If your program is ending, act before this grace period runs out.8
