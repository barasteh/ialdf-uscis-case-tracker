Executive orders & policy memoranda — USCIS adjudication pause

  • Executive Order 14161 — “Protecting the United States from Foreign Terrorists and Other National Security and Public Safety Threats” (January 20, 2025). Directed enhanced vetting.1
  • Presidential Proclamation 10949 and 10998 — Expanded travel restrictions to approximately 39 countries. Iran has been included since EO 13769 in 2017.2
  • PM-602-0192 (December 2, 2025) — Hold and review on pending cases from designated countries; re-review of approvals on or after January 20, 2021.3
  • PM-602-0194 (January 1, 2026) — Expanded hold to country of birth (not just citizenship); additional countries under PP 10998.4

Pending litigation — USCIS adjudication pause

As of April 22, 2026, five federal district courts have entered orders blocking PM-602-0192 / PM-602-0194 as applied to the plaintiffs before them. The hold remains in effect nationwide for everyone else. Every court that has reached the APA merits has held that plaintiffs are likely to succeed; no court has upheld the hold on the merits. The sole adverse ruling (Osman) turned on irreparable harm and did not reach the APA merits.

The four-ground framework from Behdin

In Behdin v. Edlow, Judge Van Keulen identified four independent APA grounds, any one of which would sustain the injunction:

  1. Unreasonable delay under § 706(1) applying the six-factor TRAC framework.
  2. Unlawful withholding under § 706(1).
  3. Arbitrary and capricious action under § 706(2)(A) under State Farm and Regents.
  4. Failure to follow notice-and-comment under § 553.

The § 553 finding is the most consequential — if affirmed, USCIS would have to rescind the PMs and re-promulgate any similar policy through formal rulemaking.

For a case-by-case account of every order, see Pending Litigation on the public site.

Practice notes

  • Standalone I-131 claims are significantly harder to sustain on irreparable harm than EAD or AOS claims. Consider bundling with an I-765 or I-485 claim, as Judge Ellis permitted in the N.D. Ill. TRO.
  • Forum selection. Two of five wins are from Judge Van Keulen (N.D. Cal.); two are from D. Mass. E.D. Mich. and N.D. Ga. are the districts most likely to yield a government-favorable ruling.
  • Iran-specific risk factors. Sanctioned-industry employment, field of study, academic and professional connections, family members in Iran and their occupations, and military service history (especially IRGC). Heightened scrutiny requires “extreme consistency” across all prior filings.
  • FBI background checks now encompass all interactions with law enforcement or any government agency throughout the client’s life, both inside and outside the United States. Advise clients not to speak with federal investigators about their case or personal history without counsel present.
