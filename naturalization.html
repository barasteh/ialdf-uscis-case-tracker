Pending Litigation — USCIS Adjudication Pause

As of April 22, 2026, five federal district courts have entered orders blocking PM-602-0192 and PM-602-0194 as applied to the plaintiffs before them. The hold remains in effect nationwide for everyone else.

Updated as of April 30, 2026 · Verify against primary sources before relying on any case status.

Every court that has reached the APA merits has held that plaintiffs are likely to succeed; no court has upheld the hold on the merits. The sole adverse ruling (Osman) turned on irreparable harm and did not reach the APA merits.

A. Orders granting relief

Behdin v. Edlow N.D. Cal. · Apr 15–16, 2026 · Judge Van Keulen. Preliminary injunction covering 32 named plaintiffs (31 Iranian, 1 Sudanese), with USCIS ordered to adjudicate the I-765 applications within 180 days. May 18, 2026 compliance deadline for filings made on or before Nov 19, 2025. Most analytically developed order in the litigation.
Shokri Varniab v. Edlow N.D. Cal. · Feb 20, 2026 · Judge Van Keulen. The first preliminary injunction in the litigation. Two plaintiffs (I-485 / I-765 filers); 30-day adjudication deadline. Focused primarily on unreasonable-delay analysis under APA § 706(1). MTD hearing set for May 19, 2026.
Bowser v. Noem D. Mass. · Feb 27, 2026 · Judge Kelley. Preliminary injunction for one plaintiff with a 10-day EAD adjudication deadline — the most aggressive remedy in any pause-related case. EAD expiration treated as severe, ongoing, non-compensable irreparable harm.
Doe v. USCIS (N.D. Ill.) No. 1:26-cv-02389 · Mar 26, 2026 · Judge Ellis. Temporary restraining order covering one plaintiff’s I-485, I-765, I-131, and I-485 Supplement J. Court declined to set a short adjudication deadline, focusing instead on restoring the plaintiff’s pre-pause queue position.
Doe v. Trump (D. Mass.) No. 1:26-cv-11382 · Apr 16–17, 2026 · Judge Burroughs. Temporary stay of PM-602-0192 and PM-602-0194 as applied to the plaintiffs, entered before full briefing on TRO/PI motions. Sealed filing of plaintiff identities for compliance monitoring. A PI ruling may follow quickly.

B. The one adverse ruling

Osman v. USCIS, No. 2:26-cv-10886 (E.D. Mich., Apr. 15, 2026) — Judge Kumar. Preliminary injunction denied on irreparable-harm grounds; court expressly declined to reach the APA merits. The plaintiff sought I-131 advance parole only and could not show concrete, near-term travel necessity.

Practitioner takeaway: standalone I-131 claims are significantly harder to sustain on irreparable harm than EAD or AOS claims; consider bundling I-131 with an I-765 or I-485 claim, as Judge Ellis permitted in the N.D. Ill. TRO.

C. Key pending matters

  • Meschi v. Edlow, No. 3:26-cv-01993 (N.D. Cal.) — Hearing May 1, 2026; the court is considering consolidation of the merits trial with the PI hearing. A permanent-injunction ruling, if it issues, would carry substantially more weight than any PI ruling to date.
  • Auguste v. Edlow, No. 8:26-cv-00629 (C.D. Cal.) — PI hearing May 8, 2026; first major ruling expected in the Central District of California.
  • Nezameslami v. DHS, No. 1:26-cv-00151 (N.D. Ga.) — Under advisement since March 18, 2026 oral argument; would be the first ruling subject to Eleventh Circuit precedent.
  • Doe v. Trump, No. 1:25-cv-13946 (D. Mass.) — Under advisement since Feb. 13, 2026; admin record filed Apr. 21.
  • E.M.P.C. v. Noem (S.D. Fla.) — The only case seeking class certification (for parolees). If certified under Rule 23(b)(2), relief would extend beyond named plaintiffs — the first structural break from plaintiff-by-plaintiff orders.
  • Dorcas International Institute of Rhode Island v. USCIS, No. 1:26-cv-00132 (D.R.I.) — Joint summary-judgment briefing filed April 3; nonprofit organizational plaintiffs. A merits ruling on a complete record would carry stronger precedential weight than a PI.
  • Sarrafi v. Edlow (D.D.C.) — Government-initiated consent motion to stay filed Mar. 30 (unusual posture), suggesting a government preference to avoid an adverse D.C. Circuit ruling before the appellate picture develops elsewhere.

D. Implications

  • No court that has reached the APA merits has found the hold lawful. The remaining structural question is the scope of relief — nationwide vs. plaintiff-specific.
  • The notice-and-comment finding in Behdin (APA § 553) is the single most consequential development. Vacatur, not remand, would be the remedy if affirmed.
  • The government has not appealed any of the five orders to date — likely tolerating narrow plaintiff-specific orders to avoid creating circuit-level precedent.
  • Forum concentration: Two of five wins are from Judge Van Keulen (N.D. Cal.); two are from D. Mass. The E.D. Mich. (Osman) and N.D. Ga. (Nezameslami) are the districts most likely to yield a government-favorable ruling.
