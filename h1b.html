Green Card Holders: Conditional & Permanent Residents

If you are a permanent resident, you have the right to re-enter the United States after travel abroad, provided your green card has not expired and you do not have disqualifying criminal convictions or immigration violations.1

Your card is still valid

  • Your green card remains valid for the full period shown on the card. The pause does not invalidate or shorten it.2
  • You have constitutional due process protections. These do not go away during an adjudication pause.3
  • If your green card is expiring or has been lost, Form I-90 renewals and replacements are not subject to the pause. USCIS will process your application.5
  • There is no mandatory requirement to apply for U.S. citizenship after receiving your green card. You may hold your green card indefinitely.5

Re-review of previously approved benefits

Approvals on or after January 20, 2021

If your green card was approved on or after January 20, 2021, it may be subject to re-review and possible re-interview under the December 2025 USCIS memorandum. Immigration attorneys report that reopened cases have so far been relatively limited and have primarily involved individuals whose military service was affiliated with the IRGC, or situations where USCIS determined that insufficient information was gathered during the original interview.5

If your I-485 is pending and your underlying status is expiring

If your green card application (I-485) is currently pending and your underlying status has expired or is about to expire, you should be aware of a significant change in enforcement practice. Under prior administrations, filing an I-485 while your underlying status was valid meant you were not accruing unlawful presence while the application remained pending, even if the underlying status subsequently expired. ICE is no longer consistently following this precedent. There have been cases where Iranians with pending I-485 applications — who are not yet green card holders — were approached by ICE, detained, and placed in removal proceedings after their underlying status expired. Some were released but placed in proceedings; others were held in detention facilities. If your underlying status is expiring while your I-485 is pending, consult an immigration attorney immediately.5

What you should do

  • Renew your green card before it expires using Form I-90.
  • If you are a conditional resident (2-year green card), file your I-751 petition to remove conditions on time, even during the pause. Missing this deadline can result in loss of status.
  • Carry your green card at all times.
  • Do not travel for extended periods (6+ months) without consulting an attorney. Extended absence can trigger questions about abandonment of permanent resident status.
